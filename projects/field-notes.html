field notes and feral tales

parallel archives of berlin

Documenting Berlin through parallel perspectives, this multimedia installation weaves together human field notes and animal narratives. A central archive box contains cyanotypes, photographs, and notes, while four video screens share stories of urban species adapting to city infrastructure and climate change: a car-dwelling marten, a displaced sparrow, a territorial frog, and a season-confused hedgehog. Their stories reveal how Berlin's transformations affect both its human and non-human inhabitants.

This multimedia installation explores urban wildlife adaptation through multiple documentation methods. The piece consists of:

  • Four synchronized video screens displaying 30-second narratives from animal perspectives
  • A custom-built archive box housing cyanotype prints, field photographs, and handwritten notes
  • Curated specimens and artifacts collected during urban exploration
  • Accompanying text connecting each species' story to broader themes of climate change and urban infrastructure

The installation invites viewers to consider how Berlin's changing environment affects its often-overlooked inhabitants, creating an archive that documents both human observation and imagined animal experiences of urban transformation.
